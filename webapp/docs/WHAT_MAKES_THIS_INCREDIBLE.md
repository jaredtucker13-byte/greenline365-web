## The Features That Make It Superhuman

### 1. Property Memory
We remember every house we've ever serviced. Every piece of equipment. Every repair. Every inspection. When a new owner moves in, they inherit all of that knowledge on day one.

**Example:** Sarah buys a house. She calls about dead outlets. Our AI says: "I don't see any previous issues with that circuit in our 7 years of records‚Äîthat's actually good news. Probably something simple from the move."

Sarah thinks: *"They've been watching this house for 7 years. I trust them."*

### 2. Confidence Scoring
We track how "fresh" our data is. A panel installed 2 years ago? We're 95% confident our info is current. A water heater from 2012? We're only 45% confident‚Äîso the AI asks:

> "Our records show a Rheem water heater from 2012. Is that still the unit, or have you replaced it?"

This prevents embarrassing mistakes and keeps our data clean.

### 3. Relationship Scoring (CRS)
Every customer has a score from 0-100 based on:
- How often they call
- How long they've been with us
- Whether their calls are positive or negative
- Whether they show up for appointments

**Score 0-30:** Stranger ‚Üí Formal, professional tone
**Score 31-70:** Regular ‚Üí Friendly, uses their name
**Score 71-100:** VIP ‚Üí Casual, jokes allowed, priority treatment

The AI automatically adjusts its personality based on the relationship.

### 4. Weather Intelligence
Before offering appointment times, we check the weather. If there's a thunderstorm warning at 3 PM and the customer needs electrical work:

### 5. Smart Pivot Logic
If we don't recognize the phone number, we don't give up. We ask for the address. If we find the address in our system:

> "Ah, I found 722 South 57th Street! We've been the specialists for this home for 7 years. Are you part of the Doe family with a new number, or are you the new owner?"

**No dead ends.** The AI always finds a way to recover context.
