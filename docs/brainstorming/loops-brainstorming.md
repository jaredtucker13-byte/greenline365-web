## IMPORTANT CONTEXT

GL365 is NOT a bank. GL365 does NOT process payments. The QR Shield system charges a per-scan transaction fee to the business. The business passes that cost along to the consumer however they choose. GL365 never touches the actual payment between consumer and business.

---

## WHAT ARE LOOPS?

Curated routes of 3-5 local businesses that consumers follow in sequence. Think "pub crawl meets city guide" — but for any category. Consumers scan their GL365 QR wallet at each stop. Each scan logs the visit, triggers reviews, tracks loop progress, and earns the $0.60 transaction fee from the business.

---

## CORE CONCEPT

- Loops are themed routes (e.g., "Date Night in Ybor," "Family Fun Saturday," "Taco Tuesday Trail")
- Each loop has 3-5 stops — each stop is a GL365 directory business
- Consumer scans QR wallet at each stop
- Each scan: logs visit, triggers review prompt, writes to Home Ledger (if applicable), tracks loop completion
- Completing all stops = reward (badge, discount, bragging rights)
- GL365 earns $0.60 per scan from each business on the loop
- Businesses opt in to be part of loops — cross-promotion opportunity

---

## REVENUE MODEL

- Per-scan fee: $0.60 charged to the business at each stop
- Business passes cost to consumer via their own pricing
- GL365 never processes the actual consumer-to-business payment
- A 5-stop loop = $3.00 in transaction fees per consumer who completes it

## OPEN QUESTIONS

- Who creates Loops? GL365 curators? Business owners? Consumers?
- Can businesses request to be added to popular Loops?
- Is there a Loop marketplace where businesses bid for placement?
- What's the reward for completing a Loop? Badge? Discount at next visit?
- Can Loops expire (seasonal / event-based)?
- Do Loops have a leaderboard (most loops completed)?
- How do we prevent a bad business from ruining a Loop experience?
- Can businesses create their own "mini-loops" across their multiple locations?
